Aggieland Home Compliance Checklist

General Checklist

Weed and Grass Fence Height Parking Junked vehicle Illegal Signs Property Maintenance

It shall be unlawful for any person owning, claiming, occupying, or having supervision or control of any real property within the City to permit weeds, grass, brush, or any objectionable or unsightly vegetation to grow due to lack of vegetation management upon any such real property.

Fences and walls shall be a minimum of six (6) feet in height and a maximum of eight (8) feet. Walls over six (6) feet must obtain a building permit.

It shall be unlawful for any person or operator (other than a Police Officer or City employee acting in the course and scope of their duties) to park, stop or stand a motor vehicle: (1) in or on a yard or lawn, (2) blocking a public or private driveway or alley, a dumpster, (3) within 15 feet of a fire hydrant, (4) park facing traffic or 18 inches from the curb.

Vehicle that is: 1) wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, or discarded, or 2) inoperable and has remained inoperable for more than 30 consecutive days in private properties are junked vehicle.

Signs that are intended to or designed to resemble traffic signs, portable and trailer signs, temporary freestanding signs, signs painted on rooftops, off-premises (including commercial and non-commercial billboards) are illegal signs. However, temporary signs erected by private property owners for the purpose of warning of a dangerous defect, condition, or other hazard to the public are exempt.

Important property maintenance to avoid city code violations are primarily (but not limited to): pest control, periodic change of filters (AC, water, dishwasher, etc.), smoke and CO monitors, fire extinguisher inspection, plumbing system inspection, water heater drainage, gas and electrical systems check, and property exterior inspection.
